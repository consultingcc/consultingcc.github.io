The First Year: What Great Infant Care Looks Like

19 May 2026 by Chawndra C.

The first year of life is the most critical window of development — here's how we support it every single day.

No decision feels bigger than choosing who cares for your infant while you're away. The first year of a child's life is a period of extraordinary brain development. The environment a baby is in during this time matters enormously. At Nana's Little Elephants, our infant program is built around that understanding.

Ages 6 Weeks to 2 Years

We accept infants from 6 weeks of age. Our infant nursery has been thoughtfully designed to feel warm, calm, and secure — a space where babies can develop at their own pace, stimulated but never overwhelmed. Each infant is assigned a primary caregiver who gets to know that baby's personality, rhythms, and needs.

Mirroring Your Home Routine

We work closely with parents to mirror your baby's existing routine for bottle times, nap schedules, and play. Consistency between home and care helps infants feel secure and supports healthy sleep patterns.

"Babies are always more trouble than you thought — and more wonderful."

Charles Osgood
What a Day in Our Infant Room Looks Like
  • Music time — songs like "Itsy Bitsy Spider" and "Wheels on the Bus" with hand motions stimulate language and coordination.
  • Peek-a-boo and simple games that build object permanence and social connection.
  • Follow-the-Leader play that develops imitation skills and early social understanding.
  • Gentle tummy time and movement play to build strength and motor development.
  • Plenty of one-on-one cuddling and responsive caregiving — because love is a curriculum too.
