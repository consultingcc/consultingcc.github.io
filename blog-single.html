Day Care 101: How to Choose the Best Childcare Facility for Your Child

May 21, 2026 by Chawndra C. / 10 comments

Choosing a childcare provider is one of the most important decisions you will ever make for your child. This guide walks you through exactly what to look for.

With so many options available — daycare centers, in-home providers, preschool programs — the process can feel overwhelming. But it doesn't have to be. When you know what questions to ask and what to look for, finding the right place for your child becomes much clearer.

1. Start With Safety Basics

Before anything else, a facility must be safe. When you tour a center, look at the entry system. Is there a secure check-in and check-out process? Are outdoor play areas fenced and well-maintained? Are cleaning protocols posted and visible? A quality childcare center will be transparent about all of this without you having to dig.

At Nana's Little Elephants, every visitor checks in at the front, every classroom maintains a stable teacher-to-child ratio, and every surface is sanitized on a daily schedule. Safety isn't a checklist — it's a culture.

2. Look at Teacher-to-Child Ratios

This is one of the most important numbers in early childhood care. The lower the ratio, the more individual attention your child receives. South Carolina's minimum licensing standards set outer limits, but the best programs stay well below them. Ask the director what the ratio is in your child's age group — and then observe whether that ratio is actually maintained throughout the day.

Children learn best when they feel seen, safe, and loved. That begins with the adults in the room.

Shirley Tucker, Owner — Nana's Little Elephants CDC
3. Ask About Curriculum and Daily Routines

Quality childcare is not simply supervised free time. Look for a center that uses a recognized early childhood curriculum — something like The Creative Curriculum or HighScope — that builds literacy, numeracy, motor skills, and social-emotional development through intentional play. A solid daily schedule with consistent routines gives children a sense of security and helps learning happen naturally.

4. Observe the Teachers

Credentials matter, but so does warmth. When you visit, watch how teachers interact with the children. Are they down on the floor with them? Do they speak kindly and with patience? Do children seem comfortable approaching them? The relationship between teacher and child is the single most powerful factor in quality early childhood care.

Our Tips for Touring a Center

When you schedule a tour, go prepared. Walk through with intention — not just a quick glance. Here is what to pay attention to:

  • Is the space clean, bright, and stocked with age-appropriate materials?
  • Are children engaged — playing, laughing, learning — rather than just waiting?
  • Does staff greet you and your child warmly from the moment you arrive?
  • Are transitions (drop-off, pick-up, meals) calm and well-organized?
  • Does the director answer your questions openly and without hesitation?
  • Are families welcomed inside the center, not just dropped off at the door?

Trust your gut. Numbers and checklists only go so far. When you walk through the door, you should feel welcome. The environment should feel calm, intentional, and joyful. If something feels off — follow that instinct.
