Summer Program

An exciting, enriching summer full of learning, play, and adventure for children ages 5–12.

When school is out, the fun begins! Nana's Little Elephants Summer Program offers a structured yet exciting experience for school-age children throughout the summer months. From themed weekly activities and outdoor play to arts & crafts, field trips, and STEM projects, we keep kids engaged, active, and learning all summer long.

What's Included — A Summer Full of Discovery

  • Weekly themes with hands-on activities, crafts, and group projects.
  • Outdoor play, water activities, and physical enrichment every day.
  • STEM exploration through experiments, building challenges, and creative problem-solving.
  • Reading and enrichment activities to prevent the summer slide.
  • Field trips to local parks, museums, and community destinations.
  • Healthy daily snacks provided.

Frequently Asked Questions

What ages is the Summer Program for?

Our Summer Program is designed for school-age children ages 5–12. Children must be potty-trained and able to participate in group activities.

What are the program hours?

The Summer Program runs Monday through Friday from 6:00 AM to 5:00 PM, following the same schedule as our regular program. We are closed on all major holidays.

How much does the Summer Program cost?

We offer competitive and affordable summer tuition. A sibling discount is available for families enrolling more than one child. Financial assistance may be available for qualifying families — please contact us to learn more.

How do I register my child?

Contact us through our Contact page to reserve your child's spot. Spaces fill up quickly, so we encourage families to register early. A member of our team will guide you through the enrollment process.

Program Highlights
  • Licensed educators and caring staff supervising all activities.
  • Fun, themed weekly schedules that keep kids excited all summer.
  • Safe, clean, and stimulating facility environment.
  • Field trips and community experiences included in the program.
  • Open communication with parents throughout the summer.
