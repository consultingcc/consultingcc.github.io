Work With Us

Join a team that makes a difference every single day.

At Nana's Little Elephants Child Development Center, we believe that the people who work with our children are just as important as the environment we create. We are looking for warm, dedicated, and passionate individuals committed to nurturing the whole child — socially, emotionally, cognitively, and physically.

Working here means being part of a close-knit family where every staff member is valued, supported, and encouraged to grow professionally. We invest in our teachers through ongoing training, a positive team culture, and leadership that genuinely cares about your success.

If you love children, believe in the power of early education, and want to spend your days doing meaningful work in a joyful environment — we want to hear from you.

Supportive Culture

A leadership team that listens and a staff that lifts each other up.

Professional Growth

Ongoing training, certifications, and career development opportunities.

Meaningful Work

Every day you spend here shapes a child's future — that matters.

To apply, email your resume and a brief introduction to phase1@nanaslittleelephants.com or use our contact form. We review applications on a rolling basis.

Open Positions

Lead Early Childhood Teacher

Program: Infant Care, Toddler, Transitions, or First Steps 4K  |  Type: Full-Time  |  Hours: Mon–Fri, 6:00 AM – 5:00 PM

As a Lead Teacher at Nana's Little Elephants, you will plan and implement daily curriculum, build meaningful relationships with each child and family, maintain a safe and nurturing classroom, and collaborate with your co-teacher and director to deliver consistent, high-quality care.

Requirements:
  • Associate's or Bachelor's degree in Early Childhood Education (or equivalent CDA credential)
  • Minimum 1 year of experience in a licensed childcare or preschool setting
  • Strong knowledge of age-appropriate development and play-based learning
  • Excellent communication skills with children, families, and colleagues
  • Current CPR and First Aid certification (or willingness to obtain within 30 days)
  • Must pass a SLED background check and DSS clearance

Teacher Assistant

Program: All Age Groups  |  Type: Full-Time or Part-Time  |  Hours: Flexible, Mon–Fri

Teacher Assistants work alongside Lead Teachers to support daily classroom activities, supervise children during play and meals, and help create a warm, encouraging atmosphere where every child feels safe and seen. This is a great entry point for those beginning a career in early childhood education.

Requirements:
  • High school diploma or GED required; coursework in Early Childhood Education preferred
  • A genuine love for working with young children (ages 6 weeks – 12 years)
  • Reliable, punctual, and a strong team player
  • Current CPR and First Aid certification (or willingness to obtain within 30 days)
  • Must pass a SLED background check and DSS clearance
Apply for This Position
Afterschool Program Staff

Program: Afterschool (Ages 5–12)  |  Type: Part-Time  |  Hours: Mon–Fri, 2:00 PM – 6:00 PM

Afterschool staff lead structured homework help sessions, supervise enrichment activities, and provide a safe, supportive bridge between the school day and home. Ideal for college students studying education or individuals who enjoy working with school-age children in an active, engaging setting.

Requirements:
  • High school diploma or GED; some college coursework preferred
  • Experience working with school-age children (ages 5–12)
  • Ability to assist with homework across multiple grade levels
  • Patient, energetic, and organized
  • Must pass a SLED background check and DSS clearance
